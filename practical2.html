Practical 2: Ferns

Note: The taxonomic classification presented in this practical is arranged in the order: Class → Order → Family → Genus.

Taxonomy: Polypodiopsida, Polypodiales, Polypodiaceae, Aglaomorpha sp.

Key Features:
- Large epiphytic fern (grows on trees or rocks).
- Two types of fronds: sterile nest-like forming a basket, fertile long arching.
- Rhizomes thick, creeping, brown scales.

Habitat: Tropical forests, trunks or rocks.

Taxonomy: Polypodiopsida, Polypodiales, Blechnaceae, Blechnum

Key Features:
- Terrestrial ferns with erect or creeping rhizomes.
- Fronds leathery, sterile and fertile fronds clearly separated.
- Fertile pinnae narrower and elongated.

Habitat: Moist forest floors, stream banks.

Taxonomy: Polypodiopsida, Polypodiales, Davalliaceae, Davallia

Key Features:
- “Rabbit’s foot fern”, hairy creeping rhizomes.
- Rhizomes exposed, brown/silvery hairs.
- Fronds finely divided, delicate appearance.

Habitat: Epiphytic or lithophytic.

Taxonomy: Lycopodiopsida, Lycopodiales, Lycopodiaceae, Lycopodiella

Key Features:
- Creeping stems, upright fertile branches.
- Distinct cone-like strobili.
- Leaves small, scale-like/needle-like.

Habitat: Tropical forests, trunks or rocks.

Taxonomy: Polypodiopsida, Polypodiales, Nephrolepidaceae, Nephrolepis

Key Features:
- Long arching fronds, 1–2 meters.
- Narrow opposite pinnae; sori near vein ends.
- Creeping stolons.

Habitat: Moist shaded areas, forest edges, common ornamental.

Taxonomy: Polypodiopsida, Polypodiales, Blechnaceae, Parablechnum, Parablechnum minus

Key Features:
- Medium-sized terrestrial fern.
- Sterile and fertile fronds different.
- Simple-pinnate fronds, firm texture.

Habitat: Wet, shaded areas along streams, forest floors.

Taxonomy: Polypodiopsida, Polypodiales, Polypodiaceae, Phymatosorus

Key Features:
- Fronds often leathery with scattered round sori.
- Rhizomes creeping and covered with scales.
- Fronds may be deeply lobed depending on species.

Habitat: Lowland tropical forests, often epiphytic or on rocks.

Taxonomy: Polypodiopsida, Polypodiales, Polypodiaceae, Platycerium

Key Features:
- Known as “staghorn ferns”.
- Two types of fronds: basal fronds (shield-like), fertile fronds (forked, antler-shaped).
- Forms large clumps on tree trunks.

Habitat: Tropical rainforests.

Taxonomy: Lycopodiopsida, Selaginellales, Selaginellaceae, Selaginella

Key Features:
- Creeping or upright branching stems forming mats.
- Four-ranked leaves: two dorsal, two ventral rows.
- Strobili present in some species, producing microspores & megaspores.

Habitat: Moist shaded forest floors, mossy rocks, sometimes dry habitats.

Taxonomy: Polypodiopsida, Polypodiales, Pteridaceae, Adiantum

Key Features:
- Fan-shaped or wedge-shaped leaflets, thin and bright green.
- Black, wiry leaf stalks and rachis.
- Delicate, airy, pinnate fronds.
- Slightly lobed or wavy leaflet edges.

Habitat: Shaded, moist, humid environments; indoor ornamental in low-light areas.

Taxonomy: Lycopodiopsida, Lycopodiales, Lycopodiaceae, Huperzia

Key Features:
- Upright, tufted stems with spirally arranged needle-like leaves.
- No strobili; spores in leaf axils.
- “Miniature conifer-like” appearance.

Habitat: Montane forests, mossy environments.

Taxonomy: Polypodiopsida, Polypodiales, Aspleniaceae, Asplenium

Key Features:
- Long, simple, strap-like fronds with entire margins.
- Prominent dark midrib along frond.
- Fronds emerge in a rosette, “bird’s nest” form.
- Thick, leathery, bright green fronds.

Habitat: Humid tropical forests, shaded, moist, warm conditions.

Taxonomy: Polypodiopsida, Gleicheniales, Gleicheniaceae, Dicranopteris

Key Features:
- Fronds repeatedly forked (Y-shaped).
- Stiff, wiry, spreading, tangled thickets.
- Pinnae narrow, linear, evenly arranged.
- Rachis green to brownish, no central midrib after forking.
- Young fronds not tightly coiled.

Habitat: Tropical/subtropical, open disturbed areas (roadsides, hillslopes, forest edges).

Taxonomy: Polypodiopsida, Schizaeales, Lygodiaceae, Lygodium

Key Features:
- Fronds grow indefinitely like a vine, twining around objects.
- Leaves compound with many small leaflets along rachis.
- Leaflets thin, green, slightly lobed.

Habitat: Tropical/subtropical forests.

Taxonomy: Polypodiopsida, Polypodiales, Polypodiaceae, Pyrrosia

Key Features:
- Fronds simple, undivided, leathery.
- Lanceolate to elliptic shape.
- Upper surface green, underside brown/silvery hairs or scales.
- Sori round and scattered, no indusia.

Habitat: Epiphyte on tree trunks/branches, also rocks.

Taxonomy: Polypodiopsida, Polypodiales, Athyriaceae, Diplazium

Key Features:
- Pinnate fronds, many paired pinnae along rachis.
- Leaflets elongated, lobed/serrated margins.
- Fresh green, soft-textured fronds.

Habitat: Tropical/subtropical, damp, rich soil, high humidity, low direct sunlight.
